Personal Diary of Project "HRI in Self-Quarantine"

Project Background

Quarantine has been a routine for people who travel between different regions. So we had the idea of designing a human-machine interactive online dialogue bot in response to this situation.

Surveying and Brainstorming

Due to the current special circumstances, we have had the opportunity to receive feedback from many people who have experienced or are experiencing self-quarantine. Overall there are three important needs:

1. Random meal pairings that cannot be preferred

2. Relevant information from sources that are hard to harmonise

3. Negative emotions that are difficult to overcome

So we had a detailed discussion and came up with the most practical and workable solution for these three scenarios.

Implementation and Personal Reflection

I was focus on the part 3, which is actually about emotional support. But as AI has an inherent weakness in emotional communication, as itâ€™s difficult for reasoning and clustering human emotions due to their complexity, we finally to simulate a small expert system that could guide the user when abnormal emotions were detected.

But in fact the use of the usability test actually produced many surprises. As most of the people participating in the test were not in self-quarantine or were not in a negative mood, their way of interacting with the system actually did not match my preconceptions. Such that the changes I made later on were in response to other functional needs, such as entertainment recommendations (music, movies).

I suppose this is because the target audience is hard to find or the scenarios are hard to simulate, but I still think our original set-up makes sense. As we mentioned in the presentation, some of the solutions to negative emotions (such as the HKUST Wellness Center) are actually difficult to be known, and chatbots are a very good way to spread the awareness.

Conclusion

This was my first encounter with the NLP engine and I would find that it was actually much smarter than I thought and could do more than enough, and the training process was quite smooth too.

So besides the perspective and methodology of thinking about usability testing that the professor brought to the course, I think the experience of using RASA will be a very pleasant surprise for me during this project.
